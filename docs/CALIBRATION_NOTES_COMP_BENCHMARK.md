# Competitive Benchmark Calibration Notes

This document summarizes the findings from a light, manual-style research of competitive benchmarks for key scenarios. The goal is to ensure NexSupply's analysis results are within a realistic range compared to industry tools.

---

## 1. Scenario: Korean Shrimp Chips → US Convenience Stores

- **Lane and Product Category:** South Korea to United States, snack foods.
- **Approximate Freight Rate Ranges:**
    - **Ocean LCL:** $100 - $150 per CBM
    - **Air:** $4 - $7 per kg
- **Approximate Duty Ranges:**
    - Snack foods (HS Code 1905.90) from South Korea to the US are generally duty-free under the KORUS FTA. However, some ingredients might trigger duties, so a range of 0-3% is plausible for estimation.
- **Approximate Landed Cost Share of Retail Price:**
    - For low-cost, high-volume snacks, the landed cost is often between 20% and 35% of the final retail price. This leaves room for distribution, marketing, and retail margins.

## 2. Scenario: Korean Ramen → US Wholesalers

- **Lane and Product Category:** South Korea to United States, instant noodles.
- **Approximate Freight Rate Ranges:**
    - **Ocean LCL:** $100 - $160 per CBM (similar to snacks)
    - **Air:** $5 - $8 per kg (slightly heavier than snacks)
- **Approximate Duty Ranges:**
    - Instant noodles (HS Code 1902.30) from South Korea to the US are generally duty-free under the KORUS FTA. A 0-2% range is a safe estimate.
- **Approximate Landed Cost Share of Retail Price:**
    - Ramen is a staple with thinner margins than impulse-buy snacks. The landed cost is often between 30% and 50% of the retail price.

## 3. Scenario: Korean Cookies → EU (Germany / France / Netherlands)

- **Lane and Product Category:** South Korea to EU, confectionery.
- **Approximate Freight Rate Ranges:**
    - **Ocean LCL:** $120 - $180 per CBM (slightly higher than US routes)
    - **Air:** $6 - $9 per kg
- **Approximate Duty Ranges:**
    - Confectionery (HS Code 1704.90) from South Korea to the EU may have duties around 7-10%, plus VAT which is high in the EU (19-21%).
- **Approximate Landed Cost Share of Retail Price:**
    - Due to higher duties, VAT, and logistics costs, the landed cost for gift-worthy cookies in the EU is often between 40% and 60% of the retail price.

## 4. Scenario: Simple CN → US Consumer Goods (Electronics / Gadgets)

- **Lane and Product Category:** China to United States, low-value electronics.
- **Approximate Freight Rate Ranges:**
    - **Ocean LCL:** $90 - $140 per CBM
    - **Air:** $5 - $9 per kg
- **Approximate Duty Ranges:**
    - Many consumer electronics from China are subject to Section 301 tariffs, which can be as high as 25%. The base duty rate is often low (0-5%), but the Section 301 tariffs are the dominant factor.
- **Approximate Landed Cost Share of Retail Price:**
    - For low-cost electronics, the landed cost can be a significant portion of the retail price, often ranging from 35% to 60%, especially when air freight and tariffs are involved.
